# SHP Outbound Agent — Design Context

## Users

**Primary user (single-tenant):** Anthony Koscielecki — Regional Sales Consultant at Superior Hardware Products (Longwood, FL). Covers the 15-county CFL North Florida territory.

**Context of use (split):**
- **Desktop, between calls** — laptop / large monitor at his desk. Uses for prospect research, drafting outreach, pushing leads to Pipedrive, reviewing the pipeline kanban.
- **Mobile, in the field** — phone in his truck or on a job site. Uses for quickly looking up a prospect, copy-pasting their email/phone, checking which clusters are nearby for an unplanned drop-in visit, marking a prospect as "Customer" or "Dead" after a meeting.

**Job to be done:** Move cold prospects through outreach → research → personalized email → Pipedrive lead → site walk → deal as efficiently as possible. The app's purpose is to compress what used to take 15 tab-switches into one focused workflow.

**Emotions to evoke:** Confidence, competence, momentum. Should feel like a sharp, reliable instrument — not a toy, not a science project. Anthony should open it and feel like he's in command of his territory, not drowning in it.

## Brand Personality

**Three words:** **direct, dependable, regional.**

- **Direct** — SHP doesn't talk in marketing speak. The cold-email voice guide bans corporate filler ("circle back," "leverage," "synergy"). The interface should match: no fluff, no celebration animations, no fake encouragement.
- **Dependable** — 40 years in business (since 1986). Family-owned. The aesthetic should feel earned and stable, not trendy or experimental.
- **Regional** — Central Florida specifically. Geographic-pride is a real lever (the trip-score clusters are the heart of the app). Locale matters: county names, "in the area" framing, customer references from down the street.

**Voice references that work:**
- **Pipedrive / HubSpot** — familiar CRM patterns Anthony already navigates daily. Don't reinvent the wheel where existing patterns work.
- **Linear / Height** (refined product) — quiet, fast, type-driven, opinionated typography hierarchy. Steal restraint.

## Aesthetic Direction

**Light theme** primary. Bright, daylit, professional — the way Anthony's office looks during business hours.

**Branding constraints (non-negotiable):**
- Primary color: **SHP Red `#C8102E`** — already in the app, well-anchored to the brand
- Logo treatment: "SHP" wordmark in a red mark + "Outbound Agent" lockup
- Three pillars copy stays available where it makes sense (it's part of the brand DNA, not filler)

**Aesthetic "flavor" — Refined CRM with industrial roots:**
- **Typography**: Pair a confident humanist sans for body/UI (something with character — *not* Inter, *not* DM Sans) with a stronger weight for headings. A mono face only for technical fields (API keys, IDs, follow-up dates).
- **Color**: Warm-leaning neutrals tinted toward red (a hint of warm gray, not cool gray). SHP red used sparingly for affordances and brand moments — never for body text, never as a gradient. Segment colors stay distinct (K-12, Higher Ed, Local Gov, Healthcare) and accessible.
- **Layout**: Information density of Pipedrive, calmness of Linear. Tight rhythm. No cards inside cards. Hierarchy from typography and whitespace, not from boxes.
- **Motion**: Almost none. Quick state-change feedback (button press, draft loading). No celebration confetti, no morphing icons, no scroll-triggered reveals.

**Mobile parity:** Both surfaces are first-class. Phone view should focus on the list/cluster/research flow Anthony actually uses in the field — desk-first features (the Coach long-form templates, Settings) can compress to long scrolls.

## Anti-references — what this should NOT look like

- **AI-design slop**: dark mode with cyan/purple glow, gradient text, glassmorphism, side-stripe colored borders on cards, rounded-corner icons over every heading
- **B2B SaaS template**: identical card grids, hero metric tiles with "↗ 12%" badges, three feature cards in a row
- **Agency portfolio**: maximalist typography, scroll-triggered animations, "creative" cursor effects
- **Trendy tech bro**: monospace everything, dark green terminal aesthetic, "Built for engineers" energy

## Design Principles

1. **Function never bends to form.** Every visual change preserves existing app behavior. Not one feature regresses. The user has been explicit: "do not allow function to be affected."
2. **Density with rhythm.** Prefer Pipedrive-level information density over Linear-level airiness — Anthony works through 600+ prospects. But break the density with intentional whitespace where decisions happen.
3. **SHP red is a scalpel, not a paint roller.** The red appears at moments of action (primary CTAs, status badges where it belongs) and at the brand mark. It does not appear as background gradients, glow effects, or decorative borders.
4. **Mobile is a translation, not an amputation.** Field flows (Find list, prospect card, cluster drill-in, mark-as-customer) get first-class mobile treatment. Heavy authoring (Settings, Coach templates) gracefully linearizes.
5. **No AI tells.** The interface should look like someone made deliberate design decisions, not like Claude generated dark gradients and called it a day.
